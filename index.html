About

Hi! I am a GIS Analyst based in Kansas City with roots in environmental science and field ecology. I am passionate about using spatial analysis to solve environmental problems and promote sustainable practices, and I am hoping to expand on my education and shift my career more towards restoration ecology and watershed science.

I graduated from the University of Kansas with a degree in Environmental Studies in 2023. During my undergrad I worked as a research assistant for the Kansas Biological Survey, where I assisted in research studying soil science, specifically arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi and how it can aid in increasing biodiversity on post-agricultural lands. I also completed a capstone project mapping and developing a land management and restoration plan for the Quindaro Ruins site in Kansas City, where I combined ecological assessment with GIS to identify invasive species, evaluate habitat sensitivity, and design trail corridors. Outside the classroom I was involved in KU SEEDS Ecology Club and was a member of Omega Phi Alpha, a national community service sorority.

After graduating I managed scientific research conferences for Gordon Research Conferences in Barcelona and worked in telecom GIS. After this I spent a year solo traveling through 12 countries, gaining experiences that sharpened my problem-solving abilities and adaptability as much as any coursework. I have now been in my current role as a GIS Analyst for the City of Lenexa since July 2025, building GIS workflows and analyses that support stormwater management, infrastructure planning, and public works.

I am now actively working toward a funded thesis-based MS in restoration ecology or watershed science. My ideal program would consist of lots of fieldwork, a well-matched advisor, and room to bring my GIS and spatial analysis skills into the research design. Outside of work, I love to travel, hike, skydive, and paint!
